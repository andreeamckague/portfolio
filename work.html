Personal Brand Logo

University Of Waterloo Student2023

PROBLEM

Create a personal logo for an Arts student that embodies their coming of age, character, interests, and aspirations. Only granted one client pitch and a 5-minute client interview.

ROLE

Graphic Designer

RESPONSIBILITIES

Visual Concept Board
Client Communication
Illustration
Digital Mockups

SOLUTION

Created a visual concept board of internet images reflecting the client's three values: "independence," "observance," and "determination," and his interests in Japanese TV shows, strength training, and cryptocurrency. I identified recurring symbols and sketched iterations, then combined them into one complex image. The final design featured the silhouettes of an eye and a fist in the style of a contemporary minimal cryptocurrency logo—sharp geometric shapes with cool-colored gradients. The eye symbolized observance and the prevalent eye symbol in the client's favorite Japanese show. Additionally, the fist represented his strength training, determination, and drive for self-sufficiency.

Brand & Guidelines Book

E.V.E.2022

PROBLEM

The feminist organization "E.V.E." has encountered several complaints from volunteers regarding its name. The name "EVE," derived from the Bible, carries deeply feminine and religious connotations, inhibiting growth with its male and religiously diverse audience. This conflict has the team of directors divided on whether or not to retain the current name. To reach a more diverse audience, E.V.E. must either change its name or update its brand logos.

ROLE

Creative Director

RESPONSIBILITIES

Brand Identity Design
Brand Strategy
Graphic Design
Pitch Presenter

APPROACH & RESULTS

To prevent losing our existing audience due to a potential name change, I led the creation of a comprehensive brand guidelines book. This initiative involved redefining the mission of E.V.E., establishing the problem statement, analyzing examples of successful rebrandings without name changes, and creating a new unique colour palette and font. One key outcome was the development of a purple speech bubble logo, symbolizing advocacy and positive discourse, with an engaging and friendly font inside. I also introduced a Venn diagram theme for logo variations to symbolize E.V.E.'s value of coexistence and inclusivity. These efforts strengthed our connection with our audience and contributed to a 228% increase in our social media audience.

High-Fidelity Prototype & Short Film

University Of Waterloo2023

PROBLEM

Create two low-fidelity prototypes, one high-fidelity prototype, and a one-minute film about an object that embodies "overwhelm" for an audience of University Professors and Arts students. Must locate appropriate materials for the prototype and adapt the film for solo-filming.

ROLE

Prototype Designer
Director

RESPONSIBILITIES

Visual Concept Board
Client Communication
Illustration
Digital Mockups

SOLUTION, CHALLENGE, & LESSON

Conceptualized a film exploring a designer's overwhelm influencing their environment. Developed sketches and two prototypes of a garbage can resembling crumpled paper. Executed storyboarding, directing, and editing for the final short film production. Overcame prototype-making and solo-directing issues via researching malleable materials like wire and air-dry clay, utilizing hammers to shape wire, adapting storyboard shots for solo-filming, and makeshift camera stands. If given the opportunity to recomplete the project, I would produce an even more distressed garbage can. I learned to take more creative risks and to strive for designs that are memorable and full of character.
